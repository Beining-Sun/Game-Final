How to pronounce the dish name "西红柿 炒 鸡蛋"?

- 1. 西红柿: pronounced "See Hong Shee", meaning tomato

- 2. 炒: pronounced "Chao", meaning to stir-fry

- 3. 鸡蛋: pronounced "Gee Dan", meaning eggs

So when combined, the dish name "西红柿 炒 鸡蛋" is pronounced approximately as: See Hong Shee Chao Gee Dan

Today, we’re going to make a super popular and incredibly simple Chinese dish — 西红柿炒鸡蛋 (Tomato and Egg Stir-Fry).

First, place the skillet on the stovetop.

Turn the heat to low — just enough to warm up the skillet.

Add a bit of olive oil.

Pour in the beaten eggs.

Turn the heat up to high.

Let the eggs cook undisturbed until the bottom is golden and slightly crispy — that texture makes a big difference!

Flip the eggs.

Wait patiently for the other side to cook through.

Once both sides are done, cut the eggs into chunks using a spatula.

Turn off the heat.

Add some salt and give it a gentle mix. (Make sure there's enough — this is the only salty seasoning in the dish.)

Take the eggs out of the skillet.

Now pour in the tomato chunks.

Turn the heat back on to low.

Let the tomatoes cook until they release their juices. Avoid stirring too often — you want to keep the chunks intact.

Add the seasoned eggs back in.

Turn the heat up to high.

Stir gently. Make sure each piece of egg gets coated with the tomato juice.

Turn off the heat.

恭喜 (Congratulations)! — you've just mastered 西红柿炒鸡蛋 (Tomato and Egg Stir-Fry)!
